What the uniform looks like

The standard uniform is built around the official grey uniform shirt and the section necker worn by the group.

The shirt illustration shows the overall uniform shape, group necker and numbered badge zones rather than reproducing an official copyrighted placement chart.

Badge placement guide

  1. 1Group and council identification above the right chest area.
  2. 2World membership and national identification in the designated upper chest positions.
  3. 3Section and role insignia in the upper chest area.
  4. 4Personal Achievement Badges grouped in the designated achievement area.
  5. 5Outdoor Adventure Skills badges arranged in the designated lower-front area.
  6. 6Event and temporary crests placed only in approved temporary locations.
Official reference: Scouts Canada's support centre states that current placement charts are available through the uniform product pages and its Section Uniforms and Insignia Placement resource. View the official uniform resource →

Rovers and adult volunteers use the official grey uniform shirt with role-appropriate insignia and the group necker.

Badge placement guide
